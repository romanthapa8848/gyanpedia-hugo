Types of Vegetation in Nepal

Introduction

Nepal is known for its diverse topography and climate, which contribute to a wide variety of vegetation types. From tropical forests in the lowlands to alpine vegetation in the Himalayas, Nepal’s vegetation reflects its rich biodiversity and ecological significance.

Major Types of Vegetation in Nepal

1. Tropical Forests

These forests are found in the Terai region, characterized by warm temperatures and high rainfall.

  • Key Characteristics: Dense tree cover, rich biodiversity, and a variety of hardwood trees such as sal (Shorea robusta) and teak.
  • Significance: These forests support numerous wildlife species and are crucial for timber and non-timber forest products.

2. Subtropical Forests

Located at slightly higher elevations, these forests feature a mix of deciduous and evergreen trees.
