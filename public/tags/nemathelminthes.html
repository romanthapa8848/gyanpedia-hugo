Aschelminthes (Nemathelminthes): Diagnostic Features and Classification Up to Class with Examples

Aschelminthes, also known as Nemathelminthes or roundworms, are a diverse group of worms that play crucial roles in both ecosystems and human health. They can be free-living or parasitic, and their cylindrical body shape distinguishes them from flatworms.

Diagnostic Features

  • Body Shape: Cylindrical and elongated, with bilateral symmetry.
  • Body Cavity: Pseudocoelomate, having a fluid-filled cavity that acts as a hydrostatic skeleton.
  • Digestive System: Complete digestive system with both mouth and anus.
  • Nervous System: Simple nervous system with a ring-like brain and longitudinal nerve cords.
  • Reproductive System: Mostly dioecious (separate sexes), with internal fertilization.
  • Body Surface: Covered with a tough cuticle that protects them from harsh environments.
  • Excretory System: Simple excretory system with excretory canals or flame cells (in some species).
  • Movement: Whip-like movements due to the presence of longitudinal muscles only.
